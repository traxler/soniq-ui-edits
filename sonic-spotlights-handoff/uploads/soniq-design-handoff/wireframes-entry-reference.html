UX Design  ·  Combined Wireframe Package  ·  V7.4 · BYOC + Copy-Paste + Legal-UX

The first release.
End to end.

A combined wireframe deck for Soniq’s Momentum Builder — from the first 90 seconds on a phone, to the first session in the workspace, to the campaign rolling out, to the reasoning layer that earns the artist’s trust. The deck is launch-true: at launch Soniq has no send integration — it scores the curators the artist brings or searches for fit, drafts each pitch in her voice, and she sends from her own inbox (copy-paste / open-in-email); the trial gates at three free drafts, not at sending. v7.1 added the four legal-UX surfaces (preview-terms notice, sign-in assent, auto-renewal consent, terms re-acceptance gate); v7.2 added the Soniq Improvement opt-out. v7.4 is a consistency-and-honesty pass: the last “Soniq supplies the curators” language is gone, the Try preview now scores a curator she brings, the Day-0 surfaces show an honest near-empty state, the Workspace/Campaigns split and Context parity are locked, and the Artifacts nav item is retired. A closing roadmap traces bring-your-own → partner integration → owned catalog.

WHY A COMBINED ENTRY
The top-right "Sign in" on Land stays as-is; this screen serves both keys.
1

Returning users see what they expect. Most "Sign in" taps already have an account; the create path is one tab deeper, not competing on Land.

2

Land keeps its single decision. No second CTA above the fold — the Laser Yellow "Try with your release" stays the one loud action.

3

Account ≠ membership. "Create account" makes a free account. There's no pricing on Land, so the paid decision still lives later — at the fourth draft, where value and price sit together.

4

SSO-first, Spotify on top. Passwordless cuts the highest-friction step for an 11pm-in-the-studio user, and the Spotify credential doubles as catalog import downstream.

5

v7 · codified: the purple primary is deliberate. Auth is a utility surface — zero Laser Yellow. Yellow is reserved for decisions about the music, not the account (Rule 1, utility exception).

6

v7.1 · assent rides the action. The agreement line sits directly adjacent to every continue path (email + SSO) — the conspicuous-and-adjacent pattern courts enforce. Create-account logs a versioned acceptance (terms version + timestamp). Links are purple; the utility surface stays yellow-free. Counsel may escalate to an explicit checkbox — the layout reserves the room.

WHAT CHANGED, AND WHY
Three competing controls became one decision plus one optional offer.
1

"Continue on mobile" is gone. On a phone, tapping the primary already continues on mobile — the second button was a relabel of the first. Removing it restores one-decision-per-screen.

2

The laptop offer is now a real callout, not a stranded footnote. It's styled as informational (bordered, purple) — visible above the action but never competing for the yellow. The decision is "Find your curators"; the laptop is an offer she can take or ignore.

3

Mobile = immediate reward, laptop = deep work. "Find your curators" moves her straight into discovery on the device in her hand; the callout frames the laptop as where the full workspace opens up. Mobile never reads as second-class.

4

The card reflects the preview, not a supplied list. Sound analyzed, first pitch drafted, and the one curator she scored — a sampled score would not import here. No fabricated "12 matches."

5

Empty-workspace anxiety stays solved — honestly. The saved card and the "drafted your first pitch" line prove Soniq worked, without claiming curators she never brought.

THE FOUR JOBS OF THE WORKSPACE
Prepared work · one recommendation · a metrics snapshot · chat for everything else.
1

One hero recommendation, not a wall. The single directive — "Find curators for Dark Moon" — carries the only Laser Yellow on screen. One-decision discipline survives inside a dashboard; it's the "Hero Brief top-of-Workspace" pattern from the Action Brief doc.

2

Snapshot is an honest Day-0 state. At first entry she has brought no curators yet, so the snapshot invites the first ones rather than showing fabricated counts — and never a deflating row of zeros.

3

Chat is discoverable above the fold. A docked "Ask Soniq" bar sits over the nav so she knows she can ask for things the dashboard doesn't natively show (her "last 3 reviews" example). Full thread expands below.

4

Prepared work answers "now what?" The "Soniq prepared this while you signed up" line plus the brief turn an empty room into a desk with work already on it.

5

Atmosphere removed. Acquisition earns theatre; a working surface earns calm. Spotlights stops at the workspace door. (The richer multi-pane desktop view is the "open on a laptop" destination — retained as a companion surface.)
